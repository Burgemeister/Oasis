UNTITLED 1998 SURREALIST DRAMA

FORMAT: Adventure Game / Psychological Drama

In 1998, a lonely teen’s comforting dreams about her long-lost father turn into surreal nightmares, forcing her to confront her trauma as the fragile line between her dream world and reality dissolves completely.

Oasis, ’98

THE STORY

Oasis Thorne is an outcast. Haunted by the ghost of a father who vanished without a word, she lives in a self-imposed exile of incense, myths, and the endless repeat of Bôa’s "Duvet." Her only solace is a vibrant dream world where she can finally speak to her dad, feel seen by her classmates, and be the person she can't be in reality.

But the comfort is fleeting. The dreams begin to rot. Familiar faces twist into masks of terror, comforting memories become dangerous traps, and the nightmares start to bleed into her waking hours. As the surreal horrors of her subconscious manifest in the real world, Oasis loses her grip. She must navigate a terrifying landscape of dream logic and repressed memory to uncover the truth of why her father left and, more importantly, to learn that her own worth isn't something to be found in a dream, but something to be built in reality.

THE PROTAGONIST: OASIS THORNE

  • WHO: An 18-year-old with silver hair and elf-like features. Her bedroom is a candlelit dark academia haven.
  • WANT: To be seen, to be loved, to be enough for someone to stay.
  • NEED: To stop seeking validation from others and discover her own self-worth.
  • FATAL FLAW: Escapism & indecisiveness trap her in dreams and prevent real change.

KEY FEATURES & GAMEPLAY

  • DUAL REALITIES: Explore 1998 environments corrupted by dream logic and glitch effects.
  • NARRATIVE EXPLORATION: Examine objects and journal entries to unlock memories and dream sequences.
  • DREAM PUZZLES: Manipulate impossible landscapes representing psychological blocks.
  • CONSEQUENTIAL DIALOGUE: Conversations with dream-versions of key figures shape her path to self-acceptance.

TONE & AESTHETIC

  • VISUALS: Low-poly, PS1-era style juxtaposed with Lynchian horror and glitch distortions.
  • SOUND: 90s alt-rock, ambient synths, and haunting soundscapes anchored by Bôa’s "Duvet."
  • FEEL: Introspective, eerie, melancholic, and deeply psychological.

COMPARABLE TITLES

Life is Strange meets Silent Hill 2, with the distinct 90s psychological atmosphere of Serial Experiments Lain.

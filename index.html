PROJECT: UNTITLED 1998 SURREALIST DRAMA

FORMAT: Adventure Game / Psychological Drama

In 1998, a lonely teen’s comforting dreams about her long-lost father turn into surreal nightmares, forcing her to confront her trauma as the fragile line between her dream world and reality dissolves completely.

Oasis Thorne, '98

THE STORY

This is Oasis Thorne. Born on August 13, 1980, she lives with her mom. With pale skin, silver hair, and elf-like features, she's an outcast. Her room is her safe space—the smell of incense and impressive piles of books lit by candles make her cave a dark academia lover's dream. She mostly likes to read about witches and myths. A new song just came out—"Duvet" by Bôa—and it's on endless repeat.

Her dad hasn't been in the picture since she was six. He just left. Without a word. It left a wound that made her feel like she was never enough. Not enough to stay for. When she started high school, history repeated itself; she didn't fit in. These wounds made her introverted and conflict-avoidant. Most days, she just feels like a nobody.

Her only solace is a vibrant dream world where her father appears often. In those moments, she finally feels comforted, seen not just by her father, but even by her classmates. But the comfort is fleeting. The dreams begin to rot from the inside out. Familiar faces twist into masks of terror, and the nightmares start to bleed into her waking hours. As the surreal horrors manifest in the real world, Oasis must navigate a terrifying landscape of dream logic to uncover the truth of why her father left and, more importantly, to learn that her own worth isn't something to be found in a dream, but something to be built in reality.

THE PROTAGONIST: OASIS THORNE

  • WHO: An 18-year-old with silver hair and elf-like features. Her bedroom is a candlelit dark academia haven.
  • WANT: She longs to be seen and loved—just as she is in her dreams.
  • NEED: To learn that her worth isn't defined by others, but by herself. She needs to develop self-love and confidence to make her own choices.
  • FATAL FLAW: Her flaws are escapism and indecisiveness. She escapes the real world by disappearing into her dreams, causing her to lose track of time, life, and reality.

KEY FEATURES & GAMEPLAY

  • DUAL REALITIES: Explore 1998 environments corrupted by dream logic and glitch effects.
  • NARRATIVE EXPLORATION: Examine objects and journal entries to unlock memories and dream sequences.
  • DREAM PUZZLES: Manipulate impossible landscapes representing psychological blocks.
  • CONSEQUENTIAL DIALOGUE: Conversations with dream-versions of key figures shape her path to self-acceptance.

TONE & AESTHETIC

  • VISUALS: Low-poly, PS1-era style juxtaposed with Lynchian horror and glitch distortions.
  • SOUND: 90s alt-rock, ambient synths, and haunting soundscapes anchored by Bôa’s "Duvet."
  • FEEL: Introspective, eerie, melancholic, and deeply psychological.

COMPARABLE TITLES

Life is Strange meets Silent Hill 2, with the distinct 90s psychological atmosphere of Serial Experiments Lain.
